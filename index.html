Hello, and welcome to Matvey Polin's various scripting projects

First, a userscript to download CSV files of roll calls from both the House and Senate websites. This is helpful if you're trying to download a just recently published congressional roll call vote that has not yet been published on Voteview or Govtrack.

Second, a userscript to see likes and dislikes of YouTube videos shown in the sidebar of the YouTube website.

Third, a userscript to display voting records for Senators and Representatives. Each time a page for a recorded vote on the House or Senate websites loads, all voting records for a year are downloaded from the congressional websites. This information is then used to calculate each Senator's or Representative's votes with the other party (absolute bipartisanship), as well as whether their absolute bipartisanship is greater or less than average for members of their own party (relative bipartisanship). Their vote is then displayed in order of their bipartisanship as squares, from left to right and from top down. Dark red and dark blue squares are Nays/Nos, ordinary colored blue and red squares are Yays/Ayes, the lightest colors are Not Voting, and those colored in a tone between ordinary colored blue and red and the lightest colors are Present. Mousing over a politician will result in an orange box popping up to display their name, state, party, vote, and absolute and relative bipartisanship for the year the vote occured in. I have made sure the script works with tablets as well as with mice.
